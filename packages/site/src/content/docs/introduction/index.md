# Create vault

The Vault is a database that contains all your content, including workspaces, notes, attachments, etc.

So first setup your new Vault.

![](vault-creation.png)

Even though you can create vault with no password it is strongly recommended to set a strong password to encrypt and protect your data.

You need to remember only one password to all your content in vault.

:::note
Keep in mind that if you will forget a password, it is not possible to recover it. Nobody can, even we.	
:::

# Add your first note

You may create new note via click button or hotkey `Ctrl+N` or `Cmd+N`.

# Organize your notes

With Deepink you are flexible in notes organizing.

## Tags

Tags are powerful way to label your notes.

You can use a nested tags like `ideas/art` or `projects/fitness club`. It looks like folders, but unlike them you can assign many tags to a note. That feature let you express the notes relations.

## Stashes

You can add note to a "Bookmarks" and find it later quick in corresponding tab.

Also you may move a note to "Archive". Unlike Bookmarks, archived notes will be available only in "Archive" tab. That is useful to stash notes you no more actively use, but don't want to delete.

Also you can move notes to a "Bin". Notes in bin are in readonly mode, and will be automatically deleted permanently after some time, according to your retention policy.

## Workspaces

Sometimes you may want to have a dedicated database to separate concerns.

With Deepink you may just create a new Workspace inside your vault.

Every workspace have its own structure - a notes, tags, files, etc.

For example, you may have one workspace for personal notes, one for job notes, and yet another for drafts or researches.

You may create as many workspaces as you need, and still will have to remember only one password.
