The Deepink ergonomic is explained in the [introduction](/introduction), now let's explore the security aspect.

## Why I need to encrypt my data?

We live in age of computers. Computers contains a lot of insights about its owners.

There are people who knows this and actively exploit that fact for a corporate spying, blackmailing, hacking and phishing based on personal profiling, targeting of marketing spam, etc.

Your personal notes may just be copied among with a whole disk by random police officer in airport or in scope of insignificant investigation, and then your personal life will be analyzed, and all found insights may be used against you.

Your personal photos and other data may be stolen by some random software you've install. Did you know the "anti virus" software can send any of your files to the remote servers "with intention to detect a threats"?

Most of the note taking apps just ignore the reality, so users of such apps actually in equal situation like a child with physical diary book where they write down their real personal insights. The insights will remain "personal" only in case their parents are fair and will not read the book secretly.

## Why Deepink?

Deepink admits the reality and protects a users.

It encrypts your vault and don't make you think about details.

There are many apps that claims they protect and "encrypt" your data. Our research says they're lies and treat its users in most disgusting ways.

For example, some apps encrypts only note texts, but not attachments, so your photos will be disclosed.

Another ones means they are encrypt the sync data that sends to their server. That's good intention, but the data on your device remains unprotected and available to analysis.

In Deepink we had research over 60 note taking apps and none of them are good enough to trust your private notes to them.

Deepink is [open source](https://github.com/vitonsky/deepink), secure by design, and still for a normal people, not only a geeks.

## The credibility

In terms of security, any app that is developed not as open source is considered as security threat by many reasons.

Most important of them:
- The app may execute any code and users will never know what exactly app does. It may spy on you
- Even if app developers have no intention to spy on you, the malware code [may be injected](https://vitonsky.net/blog/2023/09/01/malware-in-browser-extensions/) via supply chain attack or because of programmers had not enough expertise and screwed up or got bribed or deceived. The end users will have no chances to detect that via source code analysis
- The community cannot analyze the code and audit the security. As result some products tries to buy users with publish the "independent audits" that is actually run on "pay to approve" basis
- The community and end users cannot even track how a known security problems have been fixed. It may lead to a situations where the known vulnerability is fixed and no more reproduces with known steps, but may be reproduced with a slightly changed steps

That is not all reasons, but the most important ones.

This is why any proprietary note taking app cannot be considered as credible and reliable.

And that's why Deepink is [open source](https://github.com/vitonsky/deepink).

Everybody are welcome to audit the code, rebuild it, contribute a patch or even new feature, and star the repo 😉
